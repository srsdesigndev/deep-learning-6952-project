Note : I ran experiments across several days to determine the optimal configurations for the 
models, and what you see in the poster reflects the results of those final successful iterations. 
I am happy with the results obtained; however, there may still be some room for further optimization, 
considering the number of diferent techniques I explored. As for the poster itself, while the 
content and structure are solid, I would like to mention that the figures 
do not fit entirely within the layout, giving it a somewhat messy appearance.

Regarding the experimentation process: the dataset was initially heavily imbalanced, 
which led to very poor metrics early on. I tried several approaches to address this  including 
adding pos_weight to the loss function to penalize the minority class, and adjusting the 
classification threshold but none of these gave satisfactory results. Eventually, 
I explored resampling strategies and found that random undersampling,
while a simple technique, worked surprisingly well and produced the 
best overall results. Oversampling was also attempted, but under sampling 
edged it out by just a margin. The full experimntal code is available here:

https://github.com/srsdesigndev/deep-learning-6952-project
